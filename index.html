Frequently Asked Questions

What is Z-Library?
Z-Library is one of the world's largest digital libraries, providing free access to millions of books, textbooks, academic papers, and articles in multiple languages. It serves as a valuable resource for students, researchers, and readers worldwide.
How many books can I download per day?
Guest users can download 5 books per day. Creating a free account increases the limit to 10 downloads daily. By linking your account with the Telegram bot, you can download up to 20 books per day. New accounts also receive a 2-week premium trial with higher limits.
What is Tor and is it safe?
Tor (The Onion Router) is a free, open-source browser that protects your privacy by encrypting and routing your internet traffic through multiple servers worldwide. It is developed by the Tor Project, a non-profit organization, and is widely used by journalists, activists, and privacy-conscious users. It is safe to use.
Why are some links not working?
Z-Library domains may occasionally be blocked or seized by authorities in certain regions. If one link doesn't work, try another from our verified list. For the most reliable access, use the Tor Browser with the provided onion links. We update our links regularly to ensure they remain accessible.
How can I tell if a Z-Library site is fake?
Fake Z-Library sites typically ask for payment, donations, or credit card information. The real Z-Library is completely free. Other red flags include excessive pop-up ads, requests for unnecessary personal information, and URLs that don't match the verified domains listed on ZLibNow. Always verify links through ZLibNow before entering any credentials.
